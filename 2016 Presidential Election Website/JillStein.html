Jill Stein's Early Life

  • She was born on May 14, 1950 in Lexington, Massachusetts.
  • Jill Stein graduated from Harvard College in 1973 and from Harvard Medical School in 1979.
  • She has two grown sons in medical school and residency training.

Jill's involvement with the Environment

  • As a physician, Jill became aware of links between toxic exposures and illnesses.
  • She helped lead the fight to clean up five coal mines in Massachusetts.
  • She also helped rewrite the Massachusetts fish advisories.

Other Facts

  • She is the founder of a Lexington, Massachusetts recycling committee.
  • Jill Stein is the co-author to two reports, In Harm's Way: Toxic Threats to Child Development and Environmental Threats to Healthy Aging.
