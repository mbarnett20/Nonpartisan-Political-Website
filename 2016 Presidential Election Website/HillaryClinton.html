Hillary Clinton's Early Life

  • Hillary Clinton was born Hillary Diane Rodham on October 26, 1947, in Chicago, Illinois.
  • Hillary was the oldest in her family and had two younger brothers.
  • She attended Wellesley College and there she actively participated in student politics.
  • She then attended Yale Law School and met Bill Clinton.
  • Hillary married Bill Clinton on October 11, 1975, at their home in Fayetteville.
  • Their daughter, Chelsea Victoria was born on February 27, 1980.

First Lady

  • In 1933 when her husband Bill Clinton became president he assigned her to head the Task Force on National Health Reform.
  • Hillary and her husband invested in the Whitewater real estate project but when there banked failed it costed the federal government $73 million.
  • Bill Clinton was impeached after the Monica Lewinsky scandal but the U.S. Senate failed to convict him so he stayed president.

Hillary in Government

  • Hillary ran for state senate of New York, won and became the first wife of a president to win public office.
  • Hillary then ran for president of the United States but surrendered after it was apparent Barack Obama was the front runner for the democratic party.
  • After Obama became president he nominated Hillary for Secretary of State.
